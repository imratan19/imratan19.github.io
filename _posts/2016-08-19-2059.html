--- layout: post title: "2059" date: "2016-08-19 23:31:01 +0530" categories: doodles description: facebook will overtake world population by 2059. here's how. image: http://blog.ratan.me/blog/assets/posts/favicon-200.jpg --- the population growth formula may be stated as:
Pyear2 = Pyear1[erate (of population growth) * time (of growth)],

where time (of growth) = (year2-year1).

the population of the world in 2004 was 6.314B and that in 2016 is 7.3B. substituting these values in the formula above, we get:

rate (of population growth)world ≈ 1.73773

similarily, the population of facebook in 2004 was, presumably, 1 and that in 2016 is 1.7B. substituting these values in the formula above, we get:

rate (of population growth)facebook ≈ 1.7716

now, substituting the respective rates of growth calculated in the formula above and equating the statements, we get:

Pworld2016[e1.73773 * (Y - 2016)] = Pfacebook2016[e1.7716 * (Y - 2016)],

where Y is the year on which the population of facebook will overtake that of the earth.

therefore, Y ≈ 2059
